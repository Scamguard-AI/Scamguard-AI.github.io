How to Know if an Email is a Scam – 7 Red Flags

Published: June 2026

Phishing emails are getting smarter. Even tech‑savvy users can be fooled. But most scam emails still share common red flags that you can learn to spot in seconds. Here are the top 7 warning signs:

1. Urgent or Threatening Language

“Your account will be suspended!” – “Immediate action required!” Scammers create panic so you act without thinking.

2. Generic Greetings

“Dear Customer” or “Dear User” instead of your real name. Legitimate companies usually personalize their emails.

3. Suspicious Links

Hover over any link (don’t click!) to see where it really leads. Scammers often use domains that look almost right – like “paypai.com” or “amaz0n.net”.

4. Requests for Personal Information

No real company will ask you to email them your password, SSN, or credit card number.

5. Attachments You Didn’t Expect

Zip files, PDFs, or Word documents can contain malware. Never open an attachment from an unknown sender.

6. Poor Grammar and Spelling

Many scams originate from non‑English speakers. Odd phrasing and misspellings are a big clue.

7. Too Good to Be True

Lottery wins, free prizes, or unexpected refunds are almost always bait.
