I Got a Fake PayPal Invoice – Here’s What to Do

Published: June 2026

You open your inbox and see an official‑looking email from PayPal: “Invoice #XYZ for $499.99”. Your heart skips a beat. Did someone hack your account?

Relax – this is one of the most widespread scams right now. Scammers use PayPal’s own invoicing system to send you a real‑looking request for money. The email itself is often legitimate (sent via PayPal servers), but the invoice is a trick.

How the Scam Works

The scammer creates an invoice on PayPal with a threatening note like “Your subscription will auto‑renew. Call 1‑800‑XXX‑XXXX to cancel”. The phone number leads to a call center where they try to steal your credit card details or install remote‑access software.

What You Should Do

1. Do not call the number. Log in to your PayPal account directly (not through the email) and check your activity. If there’s no corresponding transaction, ignore the invoice.

2. Forward the email to phishing@paypal.com, then delete it.

3. Scan the email with ScamGuard AI. Paste the full message text into our free scanner. It will highlight the urgency language, phone number, and any suspicious links – giving you peace of mind.
