Top 5 Scam Emails of 2026 (and How to Spot Them)

Published: June 2026

Scammers constantly invent new schemes, but some campaigns are so effective they dominate inboxes worldwide. Here are the five most common scam emails right now – and how to recognize each one instantly.

1. The Fake Invoice Scam

You receive an invoice for a product or service you never ordered. The scammer hopes you’ll panic and call the included number to “cancel”, at which point they’ll request payment or remote access.

2. PayPal “Payment Confirmed” Phish

A realistic email claims you’ve paid a large sum via PayPal. It includes a link to a fake login page designed to harvest your credentials.

3. Geek Squad / Tech Support Renewal

“Your Geek Squad subscription has been renewed for $349.99.” The email includes a phone number that connects to fraudsters posing as support agents.

4. Romance Scam Grooming

Long‑term emotional manipulation on dating sites. After trust is built, the scammer invents a crisis (medical bills, travel funds) and asks for money.

5. CEO / Business Email Compromise

A forged email from a senior executive urgently requests a wire transfer or sensitive documents. These are highly targeted and often succeed due to authority pressure.
